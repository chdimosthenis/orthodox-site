**Death** in Eastern [patristic](/Patristics "Patristics") literature is both the result of [Adam](/Adam_and_Eve "Adam and Eve")'s sin and the principle of sin's perpetuation in succeeding generations. Because of the Greek construction of [Romans](/Romans "Romans") 5:12, most of the Greek [fathers of the Church](/Holy_Fathers "Holy Fathers") held that the verse read, "As by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, because of which all have sinned." This leads to a theology of mortality as the mechanism of [original sin](/Original_Sin "Original Sin") and the fear of death as the motive behind particular acts of sin.
